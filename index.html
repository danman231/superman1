Frequently Asked Questions

What is the importance of 1 in mathematics?
1 is considered the basic unit of counting and measurement. It is also the identity for multiplication, meaning any number multiplied by 1 remains unchanged.
How does 1 function in equations?
In equations, 1 acts as the multiplicative identity. This means that any number or variable multiplied by 1 will remain the same.
Can 1 be considered a prime number?
No, 1 is not considered a prime number. By definition, a prime number has exactly two distinct positive divisors - itself and 1. Since 1 only has one divisor (itself), it does not meet this criteria.
